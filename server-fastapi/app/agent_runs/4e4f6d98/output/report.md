# What is an LLM?

## Definition

**LLM** stands for **Large Language Model**. It is a type of artificial intelligence (AI) system that has been trained on vast amounts of text data to understand, generate, and interact using human language.

## Key Characteristics

### 1. **Large Scale**
LLMs contain billions (or even trillions) of parameters—numerical values that the model learns during training. This massive scale allows them to capture complex patterns in language.

### 2. **Transformer Architecture**
Most modern LLMs are built on the **Transformer** architecture, introduced in the 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need." This architecture uses a mechanism called "self-attention" to understand relationships between words in a sentence.

### 3. **Pre-training on Massive Datasets**
LLMs are trained on enormous text corpora from books, websites, articles, and other sources—often hundreds of gigabytes or terabytes of text data.

## How LLMs Work

1. **Training**: The model learns to predict the next word in a sequence by analyzing patterns across billions of text examples
2. **Fine-tuning**: The base model can be further trained on specific tasks or refined with human feedback (RLHF - Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)
3. **Inference**: When you ask a question, the model generates responses token-by-token based on learned patterns

## Capabilities

LLMs can perform a wide variety of language tasks, including:

- **Text generation** – Writing articles, stories, code, emails
- **Question answering** – Providing information on diverse topics
- **Summarization** – Condensing long documents
- **Translation** – Converting text between languages
- **Conversation** – Engaging in dialogue
- **Reasoning** – Solving problems and logical tasks

## Limitations

- Can generate incorrect or "hallucinated" information
- Knowledge is limited to training data cutoff date
- May exhibit biases present in training data
- Cannot truly "understand" in the human sense
- Lack real-world experience or sensory perception

## Summary

An LLM is a powerful AI system trained on massive text datasets that can understand and generate human-like text. These models have revolutionized natural language processing and are now used in chatbots, writing assistants, coding tools, and countless other applications.
